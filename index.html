  • All airports in the world

    Airports are key hubs of global connectivity, allowing people to travel quickly and efficiently from one place to another. There are thousands of airports around the world, ranging from large international hubs to small regional airports. Large international airports, such as Heathrow in London, Charles de Gaulle in Paris, and John F. Kennedy in New York, are major stopping points for international travelers and serve millions of passengers each year. They have modern facilities, shops, restaurants, VIP lounges, and shuttle services for travelers.

    On the other hand, small regional airports generally serve more limited destinations and have more modest facilities. They are often used by local travelers for domestic flights or flights to tourist destinations.

    This graph is the representation of all airports in the world using a CSV file including their name, latitude and longitude. Regarding the map, Leaflet is an interactive map that allows for the display of the entire world.
